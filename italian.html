The Italian Game

The Italian Game is one of the oldest openings in chess and is typically one of the first openings learned by beginners. The moves 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 begin the Italian Game and demonstrates proper chess principles which is why it is so popular with beginners. The Italian typically leads to slower and more closed games but is still one of the most explored openings in chess.

Below is a list of some of the most important lines in the Italian

  • Two Knights Defense
  • Fried Liver Attack
  • Blackburne Shilling Gambit
  • Traxler Counterattack
  • Chigorin Trap
  • Evan's Gambit
  • Hungarian Defense
